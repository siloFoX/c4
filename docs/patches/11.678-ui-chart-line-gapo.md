## Indicator

The Gopalakrishnan Range Index (GAPO) is a fractal-style gauge of
the trading range. It needs the high and low, so
`ChartLineGapoPoint` carries `{x, high, low, close}` -- high and
low feed the range, the close draws the price line.

- `computeLineGapoRange(bars, period)` -- the high-to-low range
  over each trailing window: the highest high minus the lowest
  low.
- `computeLineGapo(bars, period)` -- the Gopalakrishnan Range
  Index: `ln(range) / ln(period)`. It climbs as the trading range
  widens. The period must be at least 2 so `ln(period)` is
  positive; a bar whose range is not positive is null.
- `runLineGapo` -- sorts the finite bars by x, runs the pipeline,
  and returns per-bar samples classified `expanding` /
  `contracting` / `steady` by the change in the index against the
  prior bar, the zone counts, the mean reading and the final
  reading; `ok` is false for fewer than two bars.
- `computeLineGapoLayout` -- stacks the price panel above a
  Gopalakrishnan Range Index panel on a dynamic scale, with the
  index line, a dashed mean reference line and one marker per
  classified bar.
- `describeLineGapoChart` -- an off-screen sentence summary for
  assistive technology.

## Testability

The index divides two natural logs, so it is transcendental in
general -- but `ln(range) / ln(period)` is the base-`period` log
of the range, which is an exact integer when the range is a power
of the period. The fixture uses a lookback of 2 and tunes the
two-bar ranges to 1, 2, 4, 8 and 16, so the index reads 0, 1, 2,
3 and 4. `Math.log(1)` is exactly 0 and `Math.log(2)/Math.log(2)`
is exactly 1, so those two anchors are asserted with `toBe`; the
higher powers are asserted with `toBeCloseTo`. The range series
itself -- integer max/min/subtraction -- is fully exact and
asserted with `toEqual`. The range then holds and collapses so
the expanding, steady and contracting zones all appear; a
zero-range fixture checks the null guard.
