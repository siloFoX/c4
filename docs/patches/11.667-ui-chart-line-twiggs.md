## Indicator math

Colin Twiggs' Money Flow measures buying and selling pressure by
weighting each bar's volume by where the close sits within the
bar's range.

`computeLineTwiggsClv(highs, lows, closes)` is the close location
value -- the range position in `[-1, 1]`:

```
clv = ((close - trl) - (trh - close)) / (trh - trl)
```

where the *true* range extends the bar's high and low to take in
the prior close: `trh = max(high, prevClose)`,
`trl = min(low, prevClose)`. A zero-width range reads 0. A clv of
`+1` means the close finished at the top of the range, `-1` at
the bottom.

`computeLineTwiggs(highs, lows, closes, volumes, period)` weights
each bar's volume by its clv, then smooths both the weighted
volume and the raw volume with a moving average and divides:

```
TMF[i] = SMA(clv * volume, period)[i] / SMA(volume, period)[i]
```

A positive TMF signals accumulation (buying pressure), a
negative TMF distribution. A window of zero volume is null.

### Exactness

The TMF is a ratio of moving averages -- no transcendentals --
so it is exact when the inputs make the divisions land cleanly.
The fixture closes finish at the high or the low (clv `+1` or
`-1`), and every window's volume sums to 256, so the whole
pipeline lands on exact dyadic numbers: the TMF series
`[., ., ., 1, 0.25, -0.25, -0.75, -0.75, 0, 0, 0.5, 0.25]` is
asserted with `toEqual`. The true-range extension is checked on
its own: a bar whose prior close pokes above the high pulls the
clv to `-0.5` where it would otherwise read 0.

## API

- `getLineTwiggsFinitePoints(points)` -- drop points with a
  non-finite field, or a negative volume.
- `normalizeLineTwiggsPeriod(period, fallback)` -- coerce to a
  positive integer.
- `computeLineTwiggsClv(highs, lows, closes)` -- the close
  location value per bar.
- `computeLineTwiggsSma(values, period)` -- the moving average
  of a nullable series.
- `computeLineTwiggs(highs, lows, closes, volumes, period)` --
  the TMF series.
- `runLineTwiggs(points, options?)` -- sort finite points by
  `x`, run the pipeline, return `{ series, period, threshold,
  clv, tmf, samples, twiggsFinal, bullishCount, bearishCount,
  neutralCount, ok }`. Each sample is classified `bullish` /
  `bearish` / `neutral` / `none`. `ok` is false for fewer than
  two points.
- `computeLineTwiggsLayout(options)` -- stack the price panel
  above a TMF panel on a fixed -1 to +1 scale; project the
  price path, the TMF path, one marker per defined bar, and the
  zero and trigger reference lines.
- `describeLineTwiggsChart(data, options?)` -- ARIA description.

## Component

`ChartLineTwiggs` is a `forwardRef` region (`role="region"`) with
an ARIA-linked description, a `role="img"` SVG, two stacked
panels (price on top, TMF below) with their own axis ticks and
grid, panel labels, a config badge showing the period, a
three-series legend (Price / TMF / Levels) with toggle, a
hover/focus tooltip exposing the clv, the TMF and the zone, and a
`motion-safe:animate-fade-in` entrance. It consumes
`{ x, value, high, low, volume }` points. The TMF panel is fixed
to a -1 to +1 scale, draws dashed zero and trigger reference
lines, and colours each marker by its zone. Every node carries a
`data-section` attribute.

## Distinctness

- **vs chart-line-nvi / chart-line-pvi** -- those use volume
  only as a gate to decide which bars to count. The TMF weights
  every bar's volume by where the close lands in the range.
- **vs chart-line-cmf** -- the Chaikin Money Flow uses the raw
  high/low and a sum-based window. The Twiggs variant extends
  the range to the prior close (the true range) and smooths with
  a moving average.
- **vs chart-line-vzo** -- the Volume Zone Oscillator signs
  whole-bar volume by the price direction. The TMF uses a
  graded close location value, not a binary sign.
