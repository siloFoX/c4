## Summary

New `ChartLineRavi` UI primitive at
`web/src/components/ui/chart-line-ravi.tsx` -- a pure-SVG
two-panel chart with a Range Action Verification Index panel
comparing a fast and a slow moving average spread.

## Indicator math

The Range Action Verification Index (Tushar Chande) decides
whether a market is trending or ranging by comparing two moving
averages of different speed.

- `computeLineRaviSma(values, period)` -- the `period`-bar
  simple moving average of the series, null through the warm-up.

- `computeLineRavi(values, fastPeriod, slowPeriod)` -- the
  Range Action Verification Index:

```
RAVI[i] = 100 * (SMA(close, fast) - SMA(close, slow)) / SMA(close, slow)
```

  the spread between the fast and the slow moving average,
  expressed as a percentage of the slow average. A positive RAVI
  marks the fast average above the slow (an uptrend), a negative
  RAVI below; the further from zero, the stronger the trend,
  while a reading near zero marks a ranging market. A
  zero-valued slow average has an undefined RAVI and is null, as
  are bars before the slow window is full.

### Worked fixture

The RAVI is a ratio of simple moving averages -- no
transcendentals -- so the whole pipeline is exact. The fixture
(fast 2, slow 4) is a period-4 periodic series
`[70, 150, 130, 50]`: every 4-window sums to 400, so the slow
average is a constant 100, and the RAVI reduces to
`fast average - 100`:

```
ravi  [., ., ., -10, -40, 10, 40, -10, -40, 10]
```

A flat series makes the fast and slow averages agree, so it
reads exactly zero; a zero-priced series makes the slow average
zero, so it reads null.

## API

- `getLineRaviFinitePoints(points)` -- drop points with a
  non-finite `x` or `value`.
- `normalizeLineRaviPeriod(period, fallback)` -- coerce to a
  positive integer.
- `computeLineRaviSma(values, period)` -- the simple moving
  average.
- `computeLineRavi(values, fastPeriod, slowPeriod)` -- the RAVI
  series, null through the warm-up and for a zero slow average.
- `runLineRavi(points, options?)` -- sort finite points by `x`,
  run the pipeline, return `{ series, fastPeriod, slowPeriod,
  ravi, samples, raviFinal, raviMin, raviMax, positiveCount,
  negativeCount, ok }`. Each sample is classified `positive` /
  `negative` / `zero`. `ok` is false for fewer than two points.
- `computeLineRaviLayout(options)` -- stack the price panel
  above a zero-centred RAVI panel; project the price and RAVI
  paths plus markers and the zero line.
- `describeLineRaviChart(data, options?)` -- ARIA description.

## Component

`ChartLineRavi` is a `forwardRef` region (`role="region"`) with
an ARIA-linked description, a `role="img"` SVG, two stacked
panels (price on top, RAVI below) with their own axis ticks and
grid, panel labels, a config badge showing the fast and slow
periods, a two-series legend (Price / RAVI) with toggle, a
hover/focus tooltip exposing the value, RAVI and sign, and a
`motion-safe:animate-fade-in` entrance. It consumes
`{ x, value }` points. The RAVI panel carries a dashed zero line
and sign-coloured markers (green positive, red negative, grey
zero). Every node carries a `data-section` attribute.

## Distinctness

- **vs a single moving-average overlay (vwma / smma)** -- those
  plot a smoothed price *level* on the price panel. The RAVI
  plots the *gap* between two averages of different speed, on
  its own zero-centred panel.
- **vs chart-line-pmo** -- the PMO double-smooths a rate of
  change. The RAVI is the raw normalized spread of two plain
  moving averages, with no extra smoothing.
- **vs chart-line-vhf** -- both score trend versus range, but
  the VHF measures the geometry of the price path while the RAVI
  measures the divergence of two moving averages.
