## Indicator

The Camarilla equation places eight pivot levels around the prior
bar's close. It needs the high, low and close, so
`ChartLineCamarillaPoint` carries `{x, high, low, close}`.

- `computeLineCamarillaPivots(high, low, close)` -- the eight
  Camarilla levels for one bar: each offsets the close by a
  Camarilla fraction of the range -- `range * 1.1 / 12`, `/ 6`,
  `/ 4`, `/ 2`, evaluated as the exact rationals `11/120`,
  `11/60`, `11/40`, `11/20`. R1..R4 sit above the close, S1..S4
  below.
- `computeLineCamarilla(bars)` -- the per-bar level set: each
  bar's eight levels are derived from the prior bar's high, low
  and close. The opening bar has no prior bar and is null.
- `runLineCamarilla` -- sorts the finite bars by x, runs the
  pipeline, and returns per-bar samples classified `bull` /
  `bear` / `neutral` against the R3 and S3 reversal levels, the
  zone counts and the final readings; `ok` is false for fewer
  than two bars.
- `computeLineCamarillaLayout` -- overlays the price line and the
  eight pivot level lines on a single panel, with one marker per
  classified bar.
- `describeLineCamarillaChart` -- an off-screen sentence summary
  for assistive technology.

## Testability

Each Camarilla level offsets the close by `range * 1.1 / d`. The
constant `1.1 / 12` is not exact in floating point, so the
offsets are computed as the exact rationals `range * 11 / 120`
(and `/ 60`, `/ 40`, `/ 20`). A range divisible by 120 then makes
`range * 11` divisible by every denominator, so the offsets land
on exact integers (11, 22, 33, 66 for a range of 120). The
fixture pins every bar's high-to-low range to 120, so all eight
pivot levels of every bar are exact integers asserted with
`toEqual`. The closes step up and down so the close clears R3,
breaks S3 and holds between them, making all three zones appear.
