smoothed signal line and a volatility band.

## Indicator math

The Traders Dynamic Index (Dean Malone) is a composite indicator
layered on the RSI. It has three parts, all living in RSI space:

- the **RSI line** -- the relative strength index of the close,
- the **signal line** -- a moving average of that RSI,
- the **volatility band** -- a Bollinger band on the RSI.

`computeLineTdiRsi(closes, period)` computes Cutler's RSI: for
each bar the gain is `max(close - prevClose, 0)` and the loss is
`max(prevClose - close, 0)`; the RSI is the share

```
RSI[i] = 100 * avgGain / (avgGain + avgLoss)
```

where `avgGain` and `avgLoss` are simple moving averages of the
gains and losses over the trailing `period` bars. A window with
no movement at all reads 50.

`computeLineTdiSma(values, period)` is the moving average of a
nullable series -- a window containing a null is null.

`computeLineTdiBand(rsi, period, multiplier)` builds the
volatility band: the middle is `computeLineTdiSma(rsi, period)`,
and the upper/lower sit `multiplier` population standard
deviations of the RSI above and below the middle.

`computeLineTdi(closes, rsiPeriod, signalPeriod, bandPeriod,
multiplier)` wires all three together and returns
`{ rsi, signal, bandMiddle, bandUpper, bandLower }`.

### Worked fixture

The RSI is a ratio of moving averages and the band period in the
fixture is 2 -- and the 2-bar population standard deviation is
exactly `|a - b| / 2` -- so with the right closes the whole
pipeline lands on exact dyadic numbers (asserted with `toEqual`).

The fixture closes `[20, 26, 36, 30, 40, 34, 24, 30, 40, 34]` are
chosen so every 2-bar RSI window has gain+loss total 16, hence
`RSI = 100 * gainSum / 16` is always an exact dyadic:

```
rsi    [., ., 100, 62.5, 62.5, 62.5, 0, 37.5, 100, 62.5]
signal [., ., ., 81.25, 62.5, 62.5, 31.25, 18.75, 68.75, 81.25]
upper  [., ., ., 118.75, 62.5, 62.5, 93.75, 56.25, 131.25, 118.75]
lower  [., ., ., 43.75, 62.5, 62.5, -31.25, -18.75, 6.25, 43.75]
```

(signal and band middle use the same 2-bar window in the fixture,
so they coincide; the defaults 7 and 34 keep them distinct in
real use.) Each sample is classified `bullish` when the RSI is
above the signal, `bearish` when below, `neutral` when equal or
either is undefined.

## API

- `getLineTdiFinitePoints(points)` -- drop points with a
  non-finite `x` or `value`.
- `normalizeLineTdiPeriod(period, fallback)` -- coerce to a
  positive integer.
- `computeLineTdiRsi(closes, period)` -- Cutler's RSI.
- `computeLineTdiSma(values, period)` -- the moving average of a
  nullable series.
- `computeLineTdiBand(rsi, period, multiplier)` -- the
  `{ middle, upper, lower }` Bollinger band on the RSI.
- `computeLineTdi(closes, rsiPeriod, signalPeriod, bandPeriod,
  multiplier)` -- the `{ rsi, signal, bandMiddle, bandUpper,
  bandLower }` series.
- `runLineTdi(points, options?)` -- sort finite points by `x`,
  run the pipeline, return `{ series, rsiPeriod, signalPeriod,
  bandPeriod, multiplier, rsi, signal, bandUpper, bandLower,
  samples, rsiFinal, signalFinal, bullishCount, bearishCount,
  ok }`. Each sample is classified `bullish` / `bearish` /
  `neutral`. `ok` is false for fewer than two points.
- `computeLineTdiLayout(options)` -- stack the price panel above
  a TDI panel; project the price path, the RSI and signal paths,
  the band area, one RSI marker per defined sample and the 50
  reference line.
- `describeLineTdiChart(data, options?)` -- ARIA description.

## Component

`ChartLineTdi` is a `forwardRef` region (`role="region"`) with an
ARIA-linked description, a `role="img"` SVG, two stacked panels
(price on top, TDI below) with their own axis ticks and grid,
panel labels, a config badge showing the three periods, a
four-series legend (Price / RSI / Signal / Band) with toggle, a
hover/focus tooltip exposing the RSI, signal and cross, and a
`motion-safe:animate-fade-in` entrance. It consumes `{ x, value }`
points. The TDI panel renders the volatility band as a filled
area, the signal and RSI as lines with RSI markers, and a dashed
50 reference line. Every node carries a `data-section` attribute.

## Distinctness

- **vs chart-line-rsi** -- that primitive plots the bare RSI.
  The TDI layers a moving-average signal of the RSI and a
  Bollinger band on the RSI on top, in one panel.
- **vs the line oscillators (gator / ravi / cog / pmo)** -- those
  reduce price to a single derived signal. The TDI is a composite
  of three co-plotted series (RSI line, signal line, band).
- **vs chart-line-bollinger** -- that band wraps the raw price.
  The TDI band wraps the RSI, measuring how volatile the
  oscillator itself has been.
