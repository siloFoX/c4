## Indicator

The Derivative Oscillator (Constance Brown) refines the RSI into
a momentum oscillator that swings around zero.

- `computeLineDerivativeOscRsi(closes, period)` -- Welles
  Wilder's RSI; a window with no movement reads 50.
- `computeLineDerivativeOscEma(values, period)` -- an exponential
  moving average over a series that may carry a null warm-up
  prefix. It advances `ema += alpha * (value - ema)`, so a
  constant input yields a bit-exact constant output.
- `computeLineDerivativeOscSmoothed(closes, params)` -- the
  double-smoothed RSI: the RSI run through two successive EMAs
  (classic 5 then 3).
- `computeLineDerivativeOscSignal(closes, params)` -- the simple
  moving average of the double-smoothed RSI (classic 9).
- `computeLineDerivativeOsc(closes, params)` -- the Derivative
  Oscillator itself: the double-smoothed RSI minus its signal.
- `runLineDerivativeOsc` -- sorts the finite points by x, runs
  the pipeline, and returns per-bar samples classified `positive`
  / `negative` / `zero`, the zone counts and the final reading;
  `ok` is false for fewer than two points.
- `computeLineDerivativeOscLayout` -- stacks the price panel
  above a Derivative Oscillator panel on a dynamic, zero-centred
  scale, with the oscillator line and one marker per defined bar.
- `describeLineDerivativeOscChart` -- an off-screen sentence
  summary for assistive technology.

## Testability

The RSI is transcendental for a varying price (it divides the
smoothed gain by the smoothed gain-plus-loss), so the Derivative
Oscillator is anchored two ways. First, exact anchors: a strictly
rising price pins the RSI at exactly 100, a strictly falling
price at 0, a flat price at 50; because the EMA advances by
`alpha * (value - ema)` a constant RSI passes through both EMAs
and the signal SMA unchanged, so the oscillator is a bit-exact
zero -- the end-to-end pipeline anchor. The RSI seed and one
Wilder-smoothed step are also pinned with a hand fixture whose
period-2 averages divide cleanly. Second, structural identities
verified exactly for a varying fixture: the smoothed RSI equals
the double EMA of the RSI, the signal equals the moving average
of the smoothed RSI, and the oscillator equals smoothed minus
signal.
