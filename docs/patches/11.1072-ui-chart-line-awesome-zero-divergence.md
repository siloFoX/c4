# 11.1072 -- UI: chart-line-awesome-zero-divergence primitive (v1.11.1090)

## Scope

Add a pure-SVG dual-panel `ChartLineAwesomeZeroDivergence`
component that renders the HL2 median price in the top
panel and the Bill Williams Awesome Oscillator (AO) in
the bottom panel, marking bullish (price down while AO up
-- bullish momentum reversal warning) / bearish (price up
while AO down -- bearish momentum reversal warning)
price-vs-AO direction disagreement (divergence) trigger
events with bias coloring derived from the AO slope at
the divergence-entry bar.

The Awesome Oscillator is a "zero-line" momentum
oscillator: AO = SMA(HL2, 5) - SMA(HL2, 34). It
oscillates around zero rather than within a fixed [0,
100] range like RSI, and its sign indicates whether
short-term momentum (5-bar median) is above or below
medium-term momentum (34-bar median). Divergence between
price direction and AO direction is the classical Bill
Williams reversal warning -- the underlying short-vs-long
momentum gap has reversed direction even though price has
not yet.

## AO zero divergence pipeline

```
HL2[i]  = (high[i] + low[i]) / 2
AO[i]   = SMA(HL2, fastLength)[i] -
          SMA(HL2, slowLength)[i]

priceUp     = HL2[i]  > HL2[i-1]
priceDown   = HL2[i]  < HL2[i-1]
aoUp        = AO[i]   > AO[i-1]
aoDown      = AO[i]   < AO[i-1]

regime :
  'aligned-bullish'   when priceUp   && aoUp
  'aligned-bearish'   when priceDown && aoDown
  'divergent-bullish' when priceDown && aoUp
                      (bullish momentum reversal warning)
  'divergent-bearish' when priceUp   && aoDown
                      (bearish momentum reversal warning)
  'none'              otherwise (null or any flat)

bullish cross :
  prev regime !== 'divergent-bullish' &&
  cur regime === 'divergent-bullish'
bearish cross :
  prev regime !== 'divergent-bearish' &&
  cur regime === 'divergent-bearish'

bias : AO[i] vs AO[i-1] -> up/down/flat/none
```

Default `fastLength = 5`, `slowLength = 34` -- canonical
Bill Williams Awesome Oscillator tuning.

## Sibling family (divergence-cross family)

Seven members now:

- **chart-line-atr-divergence-cross v1.11.1046** --
  price vs ATR (volatility direction)
- **chart-line-keltner-divergence-cross v1.11.1054** --
  price vs Keltner mid
- **chart-line-bollinger-divergence-cross v1.11.1055** --
  price vs Bollinger mid
- **chart-line-donchian-divergence-cross v1.11.1059** --
  price vs Donchian mid
- **chart-line-momentum-divergence-cross v1.11.1088** --
  price vs raw Momentum oscillator
- **chart-line-roc-divergence-cross v1.11.1089** --
  price vs ROC percentage oscillator
- **chart-line-awesome-zero-divergence v1.11.1090**
  (this primitive) -- price vs Bill Williams Awesome
  Oscillator (the classical zero-line momentum reversal
  warning)

Distinct from the Momentum / ROC siblings: AO is a
DUAL-SMA difference oscillator (5-bar fast SMA minus
34-bar slow SMA of HL2), not a single-period delta. This
construction means AO has bit-exact CONSTANT behaviour on
linear input (14.5 on LINEAR UP, -14.5 on LINEAR DOWN) --
similar to Momentum's behaviour, but the constant value
is the half-difference between the two SMA lookbacks
(slowLength - fastLength) / 2 rather than the raw
Momentum period.

Warmup is `slowLength = 34` for the first AO value: the
slow SMA window fills at i = slowLength - 1. Cross
detection needs the previous bar's regime, so the first
potential cross lands at i = slowLength = 34.

## Bit-exact anchors

All anchors use HL input.

- **CONST band** `high = K + 1`, `low = K - 1`: HL2 =
  K (constant). SMA(K, 5) = K, SMA(K, 34) = K. AO =
  K - K = 0 (constant from i = slowLength - 1). Price
  delta = 0 (flat), AO delta = 0 (flat). regime `none`
  throughout. 0 crosses. Verified across K in {0, 1,
  50, 200, 1234}.
- **LINEAR UP** `high = i + 1`, `low = i - 1`: HL2 =
  i. SMA(HL2, 5)[i] = i - 2 (mean of i-4..i),
  SMA(HL2, 34)[i] = i - 16.5 (mean of i-33..i). AO[i]
  = (i - 2) - (i - 16.5) = **14.5** (constant). Price
  delta = +1 (priceUp). AO delta = 0 (flat). regime
  `none`. 0 crosses.
- **LINEAR DOWN** `high = -i + 1`, `low = -i - 1`:
  HL2 = -i. AO[i] = **-14.5** (constant mirror). regime
  `none`. 0 crosses.
- **QUADRATIC UP** `high = i*i + 1`, `low = i*i - 1`:
  HL2 = i*i. The 5-bar SMA of i^2 grows faster than
  the 34-bar SMA of i^2 (smaller window captures more
  recent acceleration). AO rises monotonically.
  priceUp + aoUp -> aligned-bullish. 0 crosses.
- **DECELERATING DECLINE** `high = (100 - sqrt(i+1)) +
  1`, `low = (100 - sqrt(i+1)) - 1`: HL2 = 100 -
  sqrt(i+1). The 5-bar SMA tracks the recent
  decelerating segment more closely than the 34-bar
  SMA, so the AO eventually starts rising (fast SMA
  rising toward slow SMA from below). priceDown + aoUp
  -> divergent-bullish + at least one bullish cross.

The constant-on-linear-input property (AO = 14.5 / AO =
-14.5) is a unique bit-exact characteristic of the AO
that follows from its dual-SMA construction: any input
where the per-bar delta is constant produces a constant
fast-vs-slow SMA gap, regardless of the absolute price
level. This is shared with Momentum (also constant on
linear) and distinct from ROC (always divergent against
linear input due to percentage normalisation).

## Risk

- Pure-SVG, deterministic `.toFixed(2)`, no canvas, no
  chart lib dependency.
- 5-state regime classifier follows the divergence-
  cross family convention exactly.
- HL input shape distinguishes this primitive from
  Momentum / ROC siblings (which use single-close
  input). HL2 is the canonical Bill Williams price
  proxy.
- The dual-SMA construction makes AO computationally
  heavier than Momentum / ROC (two SMA windows instead
  of one period delta) but more stable on noisy input
  (the 34-bar slow SMA dampens high-frequency
  variation).
- Strict inequalities (priceUp = `>`, etc.) -- exact
  ties produce regime `none` rather than ambiguous
  state.
- The constant-on-linear-input bit-exact property (AO =
  14.5 / AO = -14.5) is a unique signature of the
  dual-SMA construction. This makes LINEAR UP and
  LINEAR DOWN both produce 0 crosses, matching the
  Momentum sibling's behaviour. ROC sibling diverges
  here: under ROC, LINEAR UP fires bearish crosses.
- The QUADRATIC UP anchor verifies aligned-bullish
  detection without firing crosses (no transition into
  divergent state). The DECELERATING DECLINE anchor
  verifies divergent-bullish detection with at least
  one cross firing.
- Zero-visibility guarantee in layout ensures the AO
  sign is always readable; even on a series where AO
  stays positive (or negative) throughout, the zero
  line renders at oscBottom or oscTop.
- Seventh member of the divergence-cross family. Pairs
  with Momentum (single-period delta), ROC (percentage
  normalised delta), and the volatility / mid-line
  siblings (ATR, Keltner, Bollinger, Donchian).
