# 11.561 -- web/src/components/ui/chart-line-theilsen.tsx

Pure-SVG line chart with a **Theil-Sen robust median-slope
trend estimator** overlay. The raw observations render as a
dimmed line; the Theil-Sen trend renders as a solid coloured
line; an optional dashed OLS line can be overlaid for a
side-by-side robustness comparison.

## Math

The **Theil-Sen estimator** fits a straight line by taking
medians instead of least squares:

1. For every pair of points `(i, j)` with distinct x,
   compute the pairwise slope `(y_j - y_i) / (x_j - x_i)`.
   Vertical pairs (equal x) are skipped.
2. The Theil-Sen **slope** is the **median** of all those
   pairwise slopes.
3. The **intercept** is the median of `y_i - slope * x_i`
   over the points.

Because both the slope and the intercept are medians, the
estimator has a **~29.3% breakdown point** -- up to ~29% of
the data can be arbitrary outliers without moving the fit.

`ok = false` when there are fewer than 2 finite points or
every point shares the same x (no non-vertical pair exists).

## Distinct from the OLS `chart-line-trend`

`chart-line-trend` fits the **ordinary least squares (OLS)**
line -- it minimises the sum of squared residuals.
ChartLineTheilSen fits the **robust** line.

The difference is decisive on outlier-contaminated data. The
component's own test fixture demonstrates it: four points on
`y = x` plus one extreme outlier at `(4, 100)`.

- **Theil-Sen** slope = **1.0** -- the median of the 10
  pairwise slopes `[1,1,1,1,1,1,25,33,49,97]` is 1; the
  outlier is simply outvoted.
- **OLS** slope = **20.2** -- the squared-error objective is
  dominated by the lone outlier, dragging the line to a
  slope twenty times steeper.

OLS has a **0% breakdown point**: a single outlier can swing
the least-squares line by an arbitrary amount. ChartLineTheilSen
is the estimator to reach for when the data may contain
outliers and you want a trend that reflects the bulk of the
points, not the extremes.

`computeOlsFit` is exported and the OLS fit is always
computed, so the legend and tooltip can surface the OLS
slope next to the robust slope even when the comparison line
is not drawn -- the divergence is visible in text.

## Public surface

- `<ChartLineTheilSen series={...} />`.
- `series: ChartLineTheilSenSeries[]` --
  `{id, label, data, color?}`. Theil-Sen is parameter-free;
  there are no per-series tuning options.
- `olsColor` -- colour of the optional OLS comparison line.
- Toggle flags: `showAxis`, `showGrid`, `showDots`,
  `showLegend`, `showTooltip`, `showConfigBadge`, `showRaw`,
  `showTrend`, `showOlsComparison`, `animate`.
- Controlled + uncontrolled visibility.
- Standard sizing + format + a11y props.
- `formatValue` / `formatX` / `formatCoefficient`.
- `onPointClick({series, point})`.

## Pure helpers

- `getLineTheilSenDefaultColor(index)`.
- `getLineTheilSenFinitePoints(points)`.
- `computeLineTheilSenMedian(values)` -- median; drops
  non-finite; NaN for empty.
- `computeTheilSenSlopes(points)` -- every pairwise slope
  (vertical pairs skipped).
- `computeTheilSenFit(points)` -- the robust fit:
  `{ok, slope, intercept, pairCount, slopeMin, slopeMax}`.
- `computeOlsFit(points)` -- the ordinary least-squares fit,
  for the comparison overlay.
- `runLineTheilSen(points)` -- sorts ascending, computes
  both fits, reports the x range.
- `computeLineTheilSenLayout({...})` -- projects raw paths
  and the Theil-Sen / OLS trend-line endpoints. `includeOls`
  controls whether the OLS endpoints widen the y range.
- `describeLineTheilSenChart(series, options?)`.

## ARIA + test selectors

- Root `role="region"` + `aria-label` + `aria-describedby`.
- SVG `role="img"`.
- Raw path + Theil-Sen trend line + every dot
  `role="graphics-symbol"` + `tabIndex={0}`. The trend
  aria-label includes the robust slope.
- Every node carries `data-section="chart-line-theilsen-..."`.
- Root mirrors `data-series-count`,
  `data-visible-series-count`, `data-total-points`,
  `data-slope`, `data-intercept`, `data-ols-slope`,
  `data-fit-ok`, `data-animate`.
- Series groups expose `data-series-slope`,
  `data-series-intercept`, `data-series-pair-count`,
  `data-series-ols-slope`, `data-series-fit-ok`,
  `data-series-finite-count`.
- Raw path `data-kind="raw"`; trend line
  `data-kind="theil-sen"`; OLS line `data-kind="ols"`. Dots
  expose `data-x`, `data-y`.

## Tooltip / badge

Hovering a dot shows the series label, x, y, the bold trend
value (the Theil-Sen line at that x), and the residual
(`y - trend`, with `+`/`-` prefix). Config badge:
`TS m=<slope> b=<intercept> n=<pairCount>`. Legend shows the
robust slope alongside the OLS slope per series.
