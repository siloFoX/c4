## Summary

New `ChartLineMedian` UI primitive in
`web/src/components/ui/chart-line-median.tsx` -- a pure-SVG single-panel
line chart with a **Median Price** overlay from the midpoint of the bar
high and low. The close line and the Median Price line share one panel.

## The computation

The Median Price is the midpoint of each bar's high and low --
`(high + low) / 2`. Unlike the Typical Price (11.688) or the Weighted Close
(11.689) it ignores the close entirely -- it is the pure geometric center
of the bar's range. It works on an OHLC-like bar, so `ChartLineMedianPoint`
carries `{x, high, low, close}` (the close feeds the overlaid close line,
not the Median Price).

- `computeLineMedian(bars)` -- the Median Price per bar. It reads only the
  high and low, never the close; it is purely per-bar with no lookback, so
  it is defined for every bar; a bar with a non-finite high or low yields
  null.

Each bar is classified by where the Median Price sits relative to the
close: **above** (the close finished in the lower half of its range),
**below** (the close finished in the upper half) or **equal**.

## Worked fixture

The Median Price is `(high + low) / 2`, an exact integer whenever the high
and low share parity. The fixture uses 8 OHLC bars built that way:

```
bar      high low close  median  zone
1         20  10   15      15    equal
2         24  16   24      20    below
3         30  14   14      22    above
4         36  24   30      30    equal
5         40  28   40      34    below
6         44  20   20      32    above
7         50  38   50      44    below
8         54  30   32      42    above
```

The Median Price series `[15,20,22,30,34,32,44,42]` is asserted with
`toEqual`. The close sits level with the Median Price (bars 1, 4), below it
(bars 3, 6, 8) and above it (bars 2, 5, 7), so all three zones are
exercised: 3 above, 3 below, 2 equal; final Median Price 42, final close
32. A dedicated test passes two bars with the same high and low but a
different close and asserts an identical median, proving the Median Price
is close-independent.

## API

`data` (a `ChartLineMedianPoint[]`), sizing/format/a11y props, `strokeWidth`
/ `dotRadius`, the colour props, the toggle flags (`showAxis` / `showGrid` /
`showDots` default false / `showMedian` / `showMarkers` / `showTooltip` /
`showConfigBadge` / `showLegend` / `animate`), controlled + uncontrolled
`hiddenSeries` (keys `close` / `median`), `formatValue` / `formatX`,
`onPointClick` / `onSeriesToggle`. The Median Price is parameter-free, so
there is no period prop.

Exported helpers: `getLineMedianFinitePoints`, `computeLineMedian`,
`runLineMedian`, `computeLineMedianLayout`, `describeLineMedianChart`, plus
the `DEFAULT_CHART_LINE_MEDIAN_*` constants and the `ChartLineMedianZone`
type. Every exported name carries a `LineMedian` infix so the `export *`
barrel line cannot collide.

## Accessibility

Root `<div role="region">` with `aria-label` + `aria-describedby`; SVG
`role="img"`; an off-screen `data-section="chart-line-median-aria-desc"`
description span; the close line, the Median Price line and every dot /
marker are `role="graphics-symbol" tabIndex={0}`. Every node carries a
`data-section`; the root mirrors `data-total-points`, `data-median-final`,
`data-close-final`, `data-above-count`, `data-below-count`,
`data-equal-count` and `data-animate`.
