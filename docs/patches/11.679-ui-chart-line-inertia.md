## Indicator

Donald Dorsey's Inertia smooths a vigor index with a linear
regression. It needs the open as well as the high, low and close,
so `ChartLineInertiaPoint` carries `{x, open, high, low, close}`.

- `computeLineInertiaRvgi(bars, vigorPeriod)` -- the Relative
  Vigor Index: the summed close-minus-open spread over the summed
  high-to-low range across the vigor window. It reads how
  vigorously the closes finished against their opens relative to
  the bar ranges. A window with no total range is null.
- `computeLineInertiaLinReg(values, period)` -- a rolling linear
  regression smoothing: for each bar the least-squares trend line
  is fit to the trailing window and evaluated at the endpoint.
- `computeLineInertia(bars, vigorPeriod, regPeriod)` -- the
  Inertia: the Relative Vigor Index passed through the regression
  smoothing.
- `runLineInertia` -- sorts the finite bars by x, runs the
  pipeline, and returns per-bar samples classified `bull` /
  `bear` / `neutral` by the sign of the Inertia, the zone counts
  and the final reading; `ok` is false for fewer than two bars.
- `computeLineInertiaLayout` -- stacks the price panel above an
  Inertia panel on a dynamic, zero-centred scale, with the
  Inertia line, the dashed Relative Vigor Index line and one
  marker per defined bar.
- `describeLineInertiaChart` -- an off-screen sentence summary
  for assistive technology.

## Testability

The pipeline is tested piece by piece. The Relative Vigor Index
is a ratio of summed integer spreads to summed integer ranges --
the fixture pins every bar's range to 8 and every spread to -8, 0
or +8, so a four-bar index divides by 32 and lands on an exact
eighth, asserted with `toEqual`. The linear regression is pinned
with three hand fixtures: a constant input stays constant, a
linear input is reproduced at its endpoint, and a non-linear
window resolves to its exact least-squares endpoint (2.5). The
Inertia is then checked as the exact composition of the two --
the regression of the index -- and an all-identical-bars fixture
drives the whole chain to an exact constant 0.5. The fixture's
vigor runs up then down so the regression-smoothed Inertia
crosses zero and both the bull and bear zones appear.
