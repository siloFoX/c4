## Indicator math

The Price Zone Oscillator measures how directional a price move
has been over a lookback. It is the ratio of two quantities:

- the **signed price change** -- the sum of the signed
  bar-to-bar changes, which telescopes to the net move
  `close[i] - close[i-period]`;
- the **total price change** -- the sum of the absolute
  bar-to-bar changes, the total distance travelled.

```
PZO[i] = 100 * signedChange[i] / totalChange[i]
```

Because `|signedChange| <= totalChange` always (the triangle
inequality), the PZO is bounded in `-100..+100`. A clean
up-trend, where every step is up, reads `+100`; a clean
down-trend reads `-100`; a choppy range, where the up and down
steps cancel, reads near zero. A window with no movement reads 0.

`computeLinePzoSignedChange`, `computeLinePzoTotalChange` and
`computeLinePzo` implement the three quantities.

### Exactness

The PZO is a ratio of integer sums -- no transcendentals -- so it
is exact when the closes are integers and the window total
divides cleanly. The fixture closes `[50, 52, 56, 58, 60, 58, 54,
52, 50, 52, 56, 54, 56]` with `period` 4 have an absolute-change
sequence that repeats every four bars, so every window has a
total change of 10, and `PZO = 10 * signedChange` lands on exact
multiples of 10:

```
signed [., ., ., ., 10, 6, -2, -6, -10, -6, 2, 2, 6]
pzo    [., ., ., ., 100, 60, -20, -60, -100, -60, 20, 20, 60]
```

The whole pipeline is asserted with `toEqual`.

## API

- `getLinePzoFinitePoints(points)` -- drop points with a
  non-finite `x` or `value`.
- `normalizeLinePzoPeriod(period, fallback)` -- coerce to a
  positive integer.
- `computeLinePzoSignedChange(closes, period)` -- the signed
  change over the window.
- `computeLinePzoTotalChange(closes, period)` -- the total
  absolute change over the window.
- `computeLinePzo(closes, period)` -- the PZO series.
- `runLinePzo(points, options?)` -- sort finite points by `x`,
  run the pipeline, return `{ series, period, threshold,
  signedChange, totalChange, pzo, samples, pzoFinal,
  bullishCount, bearishCount, neutralCount, ok }`. Each sample is
  classified `bullish` / `bearish` / `neutral` / `none`. `ok` is
  false for fewer than two points.
- `computeLinePzoLayout(options)` -- stack the price panel above
  a PZO panel on a fixed -100 to +100 scale; project the price
  path, the PZO path, one marker per defined bar, and the zero
  and trigger reference lines.
- `describeLinePzoChart(data, options?)` -- ARIA description.

## Component

`ChartLinePzo` is a `forwardRef` region (`role="region"`) with an
ARIA-linked description, a `role="img"` SVG, two stacked panels
(price on top, PZO below) with their own axis ticks and grid,
panel labels, a config badge showing the period, a three-series
legend (Price / PZO / Levels) with toggle, a hover/focus tooltip
exposing the signed change, the PZO and the zone, and a
`motion-safe:animate-fade-in` entrance. It consumes `{ x, value }`
points. The PZO panel is fixed to a -100 to +100 scale, draws
dashed zero and trigger reference lines, and colours each PZO
marker by its zone. Every node carries a `data-section`
attribute.

## Distinctness

- **vs chart-line-vhf / chart-line-choppiness** -- those measure
  directional strength as an unsigned ratio. The PZO keeps the
  sign of the move, so a single bounded scale reads both the
  direction and the strength of the trend.
- **vs chart-line-ravi / chart-line-cmo** -- those derive
  momentum from moving averages or summed gains. The PZO is the
  net-move-over-total-move efficiency ratio.
- **vs chart-line-roc** -- a rate of change is the raw signed
  move. The PZO normalises that move by the total distance
  travelled, bounding it.
