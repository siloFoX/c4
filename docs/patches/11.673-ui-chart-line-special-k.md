## Indicator

Martin Pring's Special K is the long-form sibling of the KST
(Know Sure Thing). Where the KST blends four momentum components,
the Special K blends twelve -- a far wider span of lookbacks --
into a single line that captures short, intermediate and
long-term momentum at once.

- `computeLineSpecialKRoc(closes, period)` -- the percentage rate
  of change `100 * (close[i] - close[i-period]) / close[i-period]`;
  bars before the lookback is reachable, and any bar whose base
  close is zero, are null.
- `computeLineSpecialKComponent(closes, rocPeriod, smaPeriod)` --
  one Special K term: the simple moving average over `smaPeriod`
  of the rate of change over `rocPeriod`.
- `computeLineSpecialK(closes, components)` -- the weighted sum of
  every smoothed component. A bar is defined only once every
  component has cleared its warm-up. The default `components`
  table is Pring's canonical twelve daily terms (lookbacks 10 to
  530, weights 1-4).
- `computeLineSpecialKSignal(closes, components, signalPeriod)` --
  the moving average of the Special K, used as the signal line.
- `runLineSpecialK` -- sorts the finite points by x, runs the
  pipeline, and returns per-bar samples classified `bull` /
  `bear` / `flat` against the signal, the zone counts and the
  final readings; `ok` is false for fewer than two points.
- `computeLineSpecialKLayout` -- stacks the price panel above a
  Special K panel on a dynamic, zero-centred scale, with the
  Special K line, the signal line and one marker per classified
  bar.
- `describeLineSpecialKChart` -- an off-screen sentence summary
  for assistive technology.

## Testability

The Special K chains a percentage rate of change (a division)
with two layers of moving average. The division is the only
non-exact step, so the fixture pins every rate-of-change base
close to exactly 100: ten leading 100s mean that for the small
custom component table every lookback reaches back into the flat
region, making `ROC = 100 * (close - 100) / 100 = close - 100`,
an exact integer. The price deltas are chosen as multiples of 6
so every moving-average window divides cleanly, and the whole
Special K series lands on integers asserted with `toEqual` (the
two-bar signal halves to an exact dyadic). The component-table
validation and zone classification are checked separately.
