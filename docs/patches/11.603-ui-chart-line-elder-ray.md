## Summary

New `ChartLineElderRay` UI primitive at
`web/src/components/ui/chart-line-elder-ray.tsx` -- a pure-SVG line
chart with an Elder Ray panel rendering bull power and bear power
around an EMA baseline. The top panel renders the close line with a
dashed EMA baseline; the bottom panel renders the bull power and bear
power lines on a symmetric y-axis with a dashed zero centerline.

This primitive consumes `{ x, high, low, close }` points.

## Indicator math

Alexander Elder's Elder Ray, with an EMA period (default 13):

```
baseline   = EMA(close, emaPeriod)
bullPower  = high - baseline
bearPower  = low  - baseline
```

`computeLineElderRayEma(src, period)` is a null-tolerant, SMA-seeded
EMA. The EMA of the close is the trend baseline; bull power measures
how far buyers can lift the bar's high above the trend, and bear
power measures how far sellers can press the bar's low below it. Both
swing around zero: positive bull power means buyers can reach above
the trend, negative bear power means sellers can reach below it.

### Worked fixture

`close = [4,4,4,12,12,12]`, `high = [10,10,10,14,18,15]`,
`low = [2,2,1,5,4,9]`, `emaPeriod = 3`:

```
ema       = EMA(close, 3) = [null,null,4,8,10,11]   (mult 0.5)
bullPower = high - ema    = [null,null,6,6,8,4]
bearPower = low  - ema    = [null,null,-3,-3,-6,-2]
```

`bullMax = 8`, `bearMin = -6`, four positive bull-power bars and four
negative bear-power bars.

## API

- `getLineElderRayFinitePoints(points)` -- drop points with a
  non-finite `x`, `high`, `low` or `close`.
- `normalizeLineElderRayPeriod(period, fallback)` -- positive
  integer; non-finite / sub-1 falls back, fractional floors.
- `computeLineElderRayEma(src, period)` -- the null-tolerant EMA.
- `computeLineElderRay(highs, lows, closes, emaPeriod)` -- returns
  `{ ema, bullPower, bearPower }`.
- `runLineElderRay(points, options?)` -- sort finite points by `x`,
  compute the series, return `{ series, emaPeriod, ema, bullPower,
  bearPower, samples, bullFinal, bearFinal, bullMax, bearMin,
  bullPositiveCount, bearNegativeCount, ok }`. `ok` is false for
  fewer than two points.
- `computeLineElderRayLayout(options)` -- stack a price panel above
  an Elder Ray panel with a symmetric y-bound. Returns panels, ticks,
  the close path, the EMA path, the bull power path, the bear power
  path, the bull and bear markers, and the zero centerline `y`.
- `describeLineElderRayChart(data, options?)` -- ARIA description.

## Component

`ChartLineElderRay` is a `forwardRef` region (`role="region"`) with
an ARIA-linked description, a `role="img"` SVG, panel labels, a
config badge (EMA period), a four-series legend (Close / EMA / Bull
Power / Bear Power) with toggle, the close line, a dashed EMA
baseline, a dashed zero centerline, the bull power line (green) and
bear power line (red), markers on each, a hover/focus tooltip
exposing the high, low, close, EMA, bull power, and bear power, and a
`motion-safe:animate-fade-in` entrance. Every node carries a
`data-section` attribute.

## Distinctness

- **vs chart-line-force-index** -- the Force Index multiplies the
  close-to-close price change by volume. The Elder Ray uses no
  volume and no price change; it measures the bar's *high* and *low*
  against an EMA baseline.
- **vs chart-line-macd** -- MACD is the difference of *two* EMAs of
  one price series. The Elder Ray uses a *single* EMA and measures
  *two* different prices (the high and the low) against it, producing
  two distinct power series rather than one difference line.
- **vs chart-line-bollinger / chart-line-keltner** -- those wrap a
  band around a moving average. The Elder Ray does not draw a band;
  it plots the signed gap from the high and from the low to the EMA
  as two independent oscillators around zero.
