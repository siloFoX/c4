## Summary

New `ChartLineBandwidth` UI primitive at
`web/src/components/ui/chart-line-bandwidth.tsx` -- a pure-SVG
two-panel chart with a Bollinger Bandwidth indicator panel
measuring band width relative to the middle line.

## Indicator math

The Bollinger Bandwidth reduces a Bollinger band to one number.

- `computeLineBandwidthBasis` -- the `period`-bar simple moving
  average (the middle line).
- `computeLineBandwidthStdDev` -- the rolling population standard
  deviation over the same window.
- `computeLineBandwidth` -- the bandwidth:

```
upper     = basis + multiplier * stddev
lower     = basis - multiplier * stddev
bandwidth = (upper - lower) / basis
```

The bandwidth is the band width expressed as a fraction of its
middle line. A low reading marks a volatility squeeze -- the
bands are tight, often the prelude to a breakout; a high reading
marks an expansion. All three series are null through the
warm-up; a flat series reports zero bandwidth; a zero basis
reports zero.

### Worked fixture

`period 2`, `multiplier 2`, price series `[5, 15, 9, 27, 45]`.
With a two-bar window the basis is the midpoint and the standard
deviation is exactly half the bar-to-bar move, so the bandwidth
lands on clean numbers:

```
[5,15]  -> basis 10, stddev 5, band [0,20],  bandwidth 2
[15,9]  -> basis 12, stddev 3, band [6,18],  bandwidth 1
[9,27]  -> basis 18, stddev 9, band [0,36],  bandwidth 2
[27,45] -> basis 36, stddev 9, band [18,54], bandwidth 1
```

The mean bandwidth is 1.5; 2 bars read wide, 2 narrow.

## API

- `getLineBandwidthFinitePoints(points)` -- drop points with a
  non-finite `x` or `value`.
- `normalizeLineBandwidthPeriod(period, fallback)` -- coerce to a
  positive integer.
- `normalizeLineBandwidthMultiplier(multiplier, fallback)` --
  coerce to a positive finite number.
- `computeLineBandwidthBasis(values, period)` -- the moving
  average, null through the warm-up.
- `computeLineBandwidthStdDev(values, period)` -- the rolling
  population standard deviation, null through the warm-up.
- `computeLineBandwidth(values, period, multiplier)` -- the
  Bollinger Bandwidth series, null through the warm-up.
- `runLineBandwidth(points, options?)` -- sort finite points by
  `x`, run the pipeline, return `{ series, period, multiplier,
  basis, stddev, bandwidth, samples, bandwidthMean,
  bandwidthFinal, bandwidthMin, bandwidthMax, wideCount,
  narrowCount, ok }`. Each sample is classified `wide` /
  `narrow` / `mid` versus the mean bandwidth. `ok` is false for
  fewer than two points.
- `computeLineBandwidthLayout(options)` -- stack a price panel
  above a bandwidth panel anchored at zero.
- `describeLineBandwidthChart(data, options?)` -- ARIA
  description.

## Component

`ChartLineBandwidth` is a `forwardRef` region (`role="region"`)
with an ARIA-linked description, a `role="img"` SVG, axis ticks
and grid for both panels, a config badge showing the period and
multiplier, a two-series legend (Price / Bandwidth) with toggle,
a hover/focus tooltip exposing the value, basis, bandwidth and
width class, and a `motion-safe:animate-fade-in` entrance. The
top panel plots the raw price; the bottom panel plots the
Bollinger Bandwidth over a zero-anchored axis, with a dashed
mean line and class-coloured markers. Every node carries a
`data-section` attribute.

## Distinctness

- **vs chart-line-stddev** -- the standard deviation band
  primitive *draws* the band (basis, upper, lower) on the price
  panel. This primitive does not draw the band; it reduces it to
  a single scalar -- the band width over the basis -- in its own
  sub-panel.
- **vs chart-line-disparity** -- disparity measures the percent
  gap between price and a moving average. Bandwidth measures the
  width of the volatility band, not the price's distance from
  it.
- **vs the oscillator panels** -- the bandwidth panel is
  anchored at zero (the bandwidth is non-negative) rather than
  centred on zero, with a mean line marking the wide/narrow
  boundary.
