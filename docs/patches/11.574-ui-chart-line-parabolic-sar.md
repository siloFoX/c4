# 11.574 -- web/src/components/ui/chart-line-parabolic-sar.tsx

Pure-SVG line chart with a **Parabolic SAR** (Stop And
Reverse) overlay -- Welles Wilder's trend-following
trailing-stop indicator. A dot is plotted each period: when
the dots sit below the price the trend is up, when they sit
above it the trend is down, and when price pierces the dot
the trend "stops and reverses".

## The Parabolic SAR algorithm

`runLineParabolicSar(points, options)` adapts Wilder's
algorithm to a single-value series:

1. The initial trend is taken from the first move (up if the
   second value is at or above the first, else down).
2. Each period the SAR advances toward the **extreme point**
   (`ep`) by an acceleration factor (`af`):
   `sar += af * (ep - sar)`. The factor starts at `step`
   (default 0.02) and grows by `step` -- capped at `maxStep`
   (default 0.2) -- every time the trend posts a new
   extreme.
3. The SAR is clamped so it cannot cross into the prior one
   or two values (it stays on its side of the price).
4. When price pierces the SAR the trend **stops and
   reverses**: the SAR jumps to the prior extreme point, the
   new extreme point becomes the current value, and the
   acceleration factor resets to `step`.

`maxStep` is clamped up to at least `step` so the band is
always well ordered.

The component fixture rises from 10 to 16 then falls to 8.
The SAR accelerates from 10 (`af` growing 0.02, 0.04, ...)
to `10.24` and `10.7008`, then at index 5 price drops to 9
-- below the clamped SAR of 11 -- so the trend flips to
down, the SAR jumps to the prior extreme point 16, and the
next period settles at `15.86`. One reversal, five up
periods, two down.

## Distinct from neighbouring primitives

The Parabolic SAR is a trend-following trailing stop with an
**accelerating** recursion and a hard stop-and-reverse flip
-- no existing primitive computes it. `chart-line-zigzag`
filters swing pivots by a percent reversal (a static
geometric filter, not an accelerating stop);
`chart-line-keltner` / `chart-line-bollinger` draw
volatility bands; `chart-line-trend` fits a regression
line. The SAR's dots are a per-period stop level that
parabolically chases the price.

All exported helpers carry a `LineParabolicSar` infix so the
`export *` barrel line cannot collide.

## Public surface

- `<ChartLineParabolicSar data={...} />` -- a single
  `ChartLineParabolicSarPoint[]` (`{x, value}`).
- `step` / `maxStep` -- the acceleration-factor increment
  and cap (defaults 0.02 / 0.2).
- Toggle flags: `showAxis`, `showGrid`, `showDots` (default
  false -- the price dots), `showSar`, `showReversals`,
  `showTooltip`, `showConfigBadge`, `showFooter`, `animate`.
- Standard sizing + format + a11y props. `formatValue` /
  `formatX`.
- `onSarClick({sample})`.

## Pure helpers

- `getLineParabolicSarFinitePoints(points)`.
- `normalizeLineParabolicSarStep(value, fallback)`.
- `runLineParabolicSar(points, options?)` -- the SAR
  recursion; reports the per-period samples plus the
  reversal / up / down counts.
- `computeLineParabolicSarLayout({...})` -- projects the
  price line, the price dots and the SAR dots.
- `describeLineParabolicSarChart(data, options?)`.

## ARIA + test selectors

- Root `role="region"` + `aria-label` + `aria-describedby`.
- SVG `role="img"`.
- The price line and every SAR dot `role="graphics-symbol"`
  + `tabIndex={0}`.
- Every node carries
  `data-section="chart-line-parabolic-sar-..."`.
- Root mirrors `data-reversal-count`, `data-up-count`,
  `data-down-count`, `data-total-points`, `data-animate`.
- SAR dots expose `data-trend` (`up` / `down`),
  `data-reversed`, `data-sar`, `data-value`. Reversal
  markers expose `data-point-index` and `data-trend`.

## Tooltip / badge / footer

Hovering a SAR dot shows the trend, the SAR (stop) value,
the price, and -- on a reversal period -- a "stop and
reverse" note. Config badge: `SAR af=<step> max=<maxStep>
flips=<count>`. The footer reports the step parameters, the
flip count and the up/down period tally.
