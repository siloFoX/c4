## Indicator

The Gann HiLo Activator (Robert Krausz) is a trailing-stop
indicator that flips between two moving averages. It needs the
high, low and close, so `ChartLineHiloPoint` carries `{x, high,
low, close}`.

- `computeLineHiloSma(values, period)` -- the simple moving
  average over a window; used for the highs and the lows.
- `computeLineHilo(bars, period)` -- the Gann HiLo Activator. The
  trailing stop follows the moving average of the lows while the
  trend is up and the moving average of the highs while it is
  down. The trend flips when the close crosses the stop: a close
  above the high average turns the trend up, a close below the
  low average turns it down. The result carries the high and low
  moving averages, the active stop, and the trend.
- `runLineHilo` -- sorts the finite bars by x, runs the pipeline,
  and returns per-bar samples carrying the trend and a flip flag,
  the up / down / flip counts and the final stop and trend; `ok`
  is false for fewer than two bars.
- `computeLineHiloLayout` -- overlays the price line and the HiLo
  Activator line on a single panel, with a marker on each flip
  bar coloured by the new trend.
- `describeLineHiloChart` -- an off-screen sentence summary for
  assistive technology.

## Testability

The HiLo Activator is two simple moving averages plus comparisons
and a select -- no other arithmetic -- so an integer OHLC fixture
makes every value exact, asserted with `toEqual`. The fixture
pins every high and low to a multiple of three so the three-bar
moving averages are exact integers. The closes climb, drop below
the low average to flip the stop down, then climb above the high
average to flip it back up, so the trend series, both flips and
the up / down counts are all pinned.
