## Indicator

The Psychological Line is a simple sentiment gauge -- the
percentage of recent bars that closed up. It works on a close
series, so `ChartLinePsyPoint` carries `{x, value}`.

- `computeLinePsyUpBars(closes)` -- the up-bar flags: a bar is an
  up bar when its close exceeds the prior close; the opening bar
  is null.
- `computeLinePsy(closes, period)` -- the Psychological Line: the
  share of up bars over the trailing `period` bars, scaled to
  0..100.
- `runLinePsy` -- sorts the finite points by x, runs the
  pipeline, and returns per-bar samples classified `overbought` /
  `oversold` / `neutral` against the upper and lower thresholds,
  the zone counts and the final reading; `ok` is false for fewer
  than two points.
- `computeLinePsyLayout` -- stacks the price panel above a
  Psychological Line panel on a fixed 0..100 scale, with dashed
  upper and lower threshold lines, the line and one marker per
  defined bar.
- `describeLinePsyChart` -- an off-screen sentence summary for
  assistive technology.

## Testability

The Psychological Line is a count of up bars times `100 /
period`. Choosing a period that divides 100 makes every reading
an exact integer: the fixture uses a period of 4, so the line is
the up-bar count times 25 and lands on `0, 25, 50, 75, 100`,
asserted with `toEqual`. The up-bar flags themselves are plain
comparisons and equally exact. The close climbs five bars then
falls four, so the run covers an all-up window (100) and an
all-down window (0), exercising the overbought, oversold and
neutral zones.
