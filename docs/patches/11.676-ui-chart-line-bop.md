## Indicator

The Balance of Power (Igor Livshin) gauges the tug-of-war between
buyers and sellers within each bar. It needs the open as well as
the high, low and close, so `ChartLineBopPoint` carries `{x,
open, high, low, close}` OHLC bars.

- `computeLineBopRaw(bars)` -- each bar's Balance of Power:
  `(close - open) / (high - low)`, clamped to -1..+1. A bar with
  no range reads zero. A reading of +1 means the bar closed at
  its high after opening at its low (buyers in full control), -1
  the mirror case, zero a doji.
- `computeLineBop(bars, period)` -- the signal line, the simple
  moving average of the raw Balance of Power.
- `runLineBop` -- sorts the finite bars by x, runs the pipeline,
  and returns per-bar samples classified `buy` / `sell` /
  `balanced` by the sign of the raw reading, the zone counts and
  the final readings; `ok` is false for fewer than two bars.
- `computeLineBopLayout` -- stacks the price panel above a
  Balance of Power panel on a fixed -1..+1 scale, with the raw
  line, the signal line and one marker per bar.
- `describeLineBopChart` -- an off-screen sentence summary for
  assistive technology.

## Testability

The Balance of Power is a single division per bar. Pinning every
bar's high-to-low range to exactly 8 -- a power of two -- makes
each raw reading `(close - open) / 8` land on an eighth, an exact
binary fraction; a four-bar signal SMA then divides a sum of
eighths by four, landing on a thirty-second, still exact. So the
whole pipeline is asserted with `toEqual`. The fixture runs the
close-minus-open spread across the full +8..-8 swing, including
dojis, so all three zones appear. The clamp, the zero-range bar
and the non-finite guard are checked separately.
