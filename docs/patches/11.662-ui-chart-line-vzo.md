## Indicator math

The Volume Zone Oscillator is the volume-based counterpart of the
Price Zone Oscillator. It measures whether the volume of a
lookback window leaned to the buy side or the sell side, as the
ratio of two quantities:

- the **signed volume** -- the sum, over the window, of each
  bar's volume signed by its price direction: positive on an up
  bar (`close >= prior close`), negative on a down bar;
- the **total volume** -- the sum of the raw bar volumes.

```
VZO[i] = 100 * signedVolume[i] / totalVolume[i]
```

Because each signed contribution is `+/- volume`, the magnitude
of the signed volume never exceeds the total volume, so the VZO
is bounded in `-100..+100`. When every bar in the window is an up
bar the VZO reads `+100`; when every bar is a down bar `-100`; a
window with mixed volume reads in between, and a window with no
volume at all reads 0.

`computeLineVzoSignedVolume`, `computeLineVzoTotalVolume` and
`computeLineVzo` implement the three quantities. Each consumes
`{ x, value, volume }` points, where `value` is the close and
`volume` is the bar volume.

### Exactness

The VZO is a ratio of integer sums -- no transcendentals -- so it
is exact when the volumes are integers and the window total
divides cleanly. The fixture volumes repeat the pattern
`[10, 40, 30, 20]` every four bars, so with `period` 4 every
window has a total volume of 100 and `VZO = signedVolume` lands
on exact integers:

```
signed [., ., ., ., 100, 80, 0, -60, -100, -80, -80, -20, -20]
vzo    [., ., ., ., 100, 80, 0, -60, -100, -80, -80, -20, -20]
```

The whole pipeline is asserted with `toEqual`.

## API

- `getLineVzoFinitePoints(points)` -- drop points with a
  non-finite `x`, `value` or `volume`, or a negative `volume`.
- `normalizeLineVzoPeriod(period, fallback)` -- coerce to a
  positive integer.
- `computeLineVzoSignedVolume(closes, volumes, period)` -- the
  signed volume over the window.
- `computeLineVzoTotalVolume(volumes, period)` -- the total
  volume over the window.
- `computeLineVzo(closes, volumes, period)` -- the VZO series.
- `runLineVzo(points, options?)` -- sort finite points by `x`,
  run the pipeline, return `{ series, period, threshold,
  signedVolume, totalVolume, vzo, samples, vzoFinal,
  bullishCount, bearishCount, neutralCount, ok }`. Each sample is
  classified `bullish` / `bearish` / `neutral` / `none`. `ok` is
  false for fewer than two points.
- `computeLineVzoLayout(options)` -- stack the price panel above
  a VZO panel on a fixed -100 to +100 scale; project the price
  path, the VZO path, one marker per defined bar, and the zero
  and trigger reference lines.
- `describeLineVzoChart(data, options?)` -- ARIA description.

## Component

`ChartLineVzo` is a `forwardRef` region (`role="region"`) with an
ARIA-linked description, a `role="img"` SVG, two stacked panels
(price on top, VZO below) with their own axis ticks and grid,
panel labels, a config badge showing the period, a three-series
legend (Price / VZO / Levels) with toggle, a hover/focus tooltip
exposing the volume, the VZO and the zone, and a
`motion-safe:animate-fade-in` entrance. It consumes
`{ x, value, volume }` points. The VZO panel is fixed to a -100
to +100 scale, draws dashed zero and trigger reference lines, and
colours each VZO marker by its zone. Every node carries a
`data-section` attribute.

## Distinctness

- **vs chart-line-pzo** -- the PZO weights each bar by its signed
  price change. The VZO weights each bar by its volume, signed by
  the price direction, so it reads buying versus selling
  conviction rather than the efficiency of the price move.
- **vs chart-line-obv / chart-line-pvt** -- those accumulate a
  running signed-volume total without bound. The VZO normalises
  the signed volume by the total volume, bounding it in
  -100..+100.
- **vs chart-line-mfi** -- the Money Flow Index weights volume by
  the typical price and reads 0..100. The VZO uses raw volume
  signed by direction and reads -100..+100.
