## Summary

New `ChartLineQstick` UI primitive at
`web/src/components/ui/chart-line-qstick.tsx` -- a pure-SVG
two-panel chart with a Qstick indicator panel showing a moving
average of the close minus open spread.

## Indicator math

The Qstick indicator (Tushar Chande) measures buying and selling
pressure from the position of each bar's close relative to its
open.

`ChartLineQstickPoint` is `{ x, value, open }` -- `value` is the
bar close (the plotted price), `open` the bar open.

- `computeLineQstickSpread(values, opens)` -- the per-bar
  close-minus-open spread, `value - open`. A positive spread is
  a bar that closed above its open (a bullish bar), a negative
  spread one that closed below it.

- `computeLineQstick(values, opens, period)` -- the Qstick:

```
Qstick[i] = SMA(close[j] - open[j], period)
```

  the `period`-bar simple moving average of the spread. A
  positive Qstick marks sustained buying pressure (closes
  consistently above opens), a negative Qstick selling pressure,
  and a reading near zero a balanced market. Bars before the
  window is full are null.

### Worked fixture

The Qstick is an SMA of a subtraction -- no transcendentals, so
the whole pipeline is exact. The fixture (`period 4`) is
hand-tuned so every spread and every moving average divides
cleanly:

```
spread  [6, -2, -8, -4, 10, -6,  2, -12,  8,  4]
qstick  [.,  .,  ., -2, -1, -2, 0.5,-1.5, -2, 0.5]
```

For example `qstick[3] = (6 - 2 - 8 - 4) / 4 = -2`. When every
bar is a doji (close equals open) the spread is zero and the
Qstick is zero; a constant spread holds the Qstick at that
value.

## API

- `getLineQstickFinitePoints(points)` -- drop points with a
  non-finite `x`, `value` or `open`.
- `normalizeLineQstickPeriod(period, fallback)` -- coerce to a
  positive integer.
- `computeLineQstickSpread(values, opens)` -- the per-bar
  close-minus-open spread.
- `computeLineQstick(values, opens, period)` -- the Qstick
  series, null through the warm-up.
- `runLineQstick(points, options?)` -- sort finite points by
  `x`, run the pipeline, return `{ series, period, spread,
  qstick, samples, qstickFinal, qstickMin, qstickMax,
  bullishCount, bearishCount, ok }`. Each sample is classified
  `bullish` / `bearish` / `neutral`. `ok` is false for fewer
  than two points.
- `computeLineQstickLayout(options)` -- stack the price panel
  above a zero-centred Qstick panel; project the price and
  Qstick paths plus markers and the zero line.
- `describeLineQstickChart(data, options?)` -- ARIA description.

## Component

`ChartLineQstick` is a `forwardRef` region (`role="region"`)
with an ARIA-linked description, a `role="img"` SVG, two stacked
panels (price on top, Qstick below) with their own axis ticks
and grid, panel labels, a config badge showing the period, a
two-series legend (Price / Qstick) with toggle, a hover/focus
tooltip exposing the close, open, spread, Qstick and sentiment,
and a `motion-safe:animate-fade-in` entrance. It consumes
`{ x, value, open }` points. The Qstick panel carries a dashed
zero line and sentiment-coloured markers (green bullish, red
bearish, grey neutral). Every node carries a `data-section`
attribute.

## Distinctness

- **vs the moving-average overlays (vwma / smma / ema)** --
  those average the price level. The Qstick averages the
  intrabar close-minus-open spread -- a sentiment measure that
  does not track the price level at all, so it gets its own
  zero-centred panel.
- **vs the oscillator panels (roofing / trendflex / reflex /
  pmo)** -- those derive their oscillator from a filter or rate
  of change of the close. The Qstick derives it from the
  relationship between two distinct per-bar inputs, the open and
  the close.
- It is a primitive that requires an `open` field on each data
  point, alongside the plotted `value` (the close).
