# 11.775 chart-line-sinewave-trend (v1.11.793)

## Summary

Pure-SVG dual-panel React/TypeScript chart primitive with the
**Ehlers Sinewave Trend** oscillator panel beneath the close.
The bottom panel plots two lines: the sine and the lead sine,
both derived from the instantaneous phase of the dominant cycle
of the detrended close. Added as the
`<ChartLineSinewaveTrend />` UI primitive in
`web/src/components/ui/`.

## Algorithm

For each bar:

```
detrender[i] = 0.0962 * close[i] + 0.5769 * close[i - 2]
             - 0.5769 * close[i - 4] - 0.0962 * close[i - 6]
I[i]         = detrender[i - 3]
Q[i]         = same Hilbert FIR applied to detrender
phase[i]     = atan2(Q[i], I[i])
sine[i]      = sin(phase[i])
leadSine[i]  = sin(phase[i] + pi / 4)
```

Bars before `i = 10` (FIR + delay warmup) are `null`. When both
`|I|` and `|Q|` are below `PHASE_EPSILON = 1e-10` the phase is
undefined and both lines yield `null` (singular trend). The
two lines act as an early reversal signal: lead sine crossing
above sine marks a likely turn up, crossing below marks a turn
down.

## Anchors

- **CONST_FLAT (close == K)**: the Hilbert FIR collapses
  within ULPs of zero (bit-exact at K = 0). Both `|I|` and
  `|Q|` fall below the phase epsilon, so the bar yields
  `null` for both `sine` and `leadSine` at every position
  past the warmup. **Bit-exact** verified across
  `K in {0, 1, 5, 10, 100, -3, 0.5}`.
- **Sine and lead sine are bounded in `[-1, +1]`** for any
  finite input (since they are `sin(.)` of a finite angle).
- **Sinusoidal input**: produces finite, distinct sine and
  lead sine readings after the warmup window (they differ by
  approximately 45 degrees in phase).
