## Indicator

The Williams Accumulation/Distribution (Larry Williams) is a
cumulative price-only flow indicator -- distinct from the
volume-based Chaikin A/D line. It measures, bar by bar, how much
the close gained or lost relative to the bar's "true range".

Unlike the close-only indicator primitives in this family, the
Williams AD genuinely needs the intrabar high and low: a close-only
series would collapse the true range machinery into the trivial
close-to-close change. So `ChartLineWadPoint` carries `{x, high,
low, close}` OHLC bars.

- `computeLineWadTrueRangeHigh(bars)` -- the higher of the bar's
  high and the prior close.
- `computeLineWadTrueRangeLow(bars)` -- the lower of the bar's low
  and the prior close.
- `computeLineWadDelta(bars)` -- the per-bar accumulation /
  distribution amount: when the close finishes above the prior
  close the gain is `close - trueRangeLow`; when it finishes
  below, the loss is `close - trueRangeHigh`; an unchanged close
  contributes zero. The opening bar has no prior close and is
  null.
- `computeLineWad(bars)` -- the running cumulative sum of the
  per-bar amount, starting at zero on the opening bar.
- `runLineWad` -- sorts the finite bars by x, runs the pipeline,
  and returns per-bar samples classified `accumulation` /
  `distribution` / `flat`, the zone counts and the final reading;
  `ok` is false for fewer than two bars.
- `computeLineWadLayout` -- stacks the price panel above a
  Williams AD panel on a dynamic, zero-centred scale, with the
  cumulative line and one marker per classified bar.
- `describeLineWadChart` -- an off-screen sentence summary for
  assistive technology.

## Testability

Every step of the Williams AD is an integer max / min / subtraction
/ running sum -- no division, no transcendental -- so an integer
OHLC fixture makes the whole pipeline exact, asserted with
`toEqual`. The eight bars are arranged so every branch fires: an
up-close with the low dipping below the prior close, a down-close
with the high spiking above it, an up-close with the low holding
above the prior close, a down-close with the high exactly at the
prior close, and two unchanged closes.
