## Indicator

The Polarized Fractal Efficiency (PFE), introduced by Hans
Hannula, scores how efficiently a price moves -- how directly it
travels from where it was to where it is.

- `computeLinePfeRaw(closes, period)` -- for each bar it forms the
  straight-line distance from the start of the trailing window to
  its end, `sqrt(priceChange^2 + period^2)`, treating each bar as
  one unit of horizontal travel. It divides that by the path
  length, the sum over the window of `sqrt(barChange^2 + 1)` --
  the total length of the zig-zag the price actually walked. The
  ratio is at most 1 (the straight line is the shortest path),
  polarised by the window's trend direction (+ for an up-window,
  - for a down-window) and scaled to -100..+100. A reading near
  +100 is an efficient up-trend, near -100 an efficient
  down-trend, near zero a choppy, inefficient market.
- `computeLinePfe(closes, period, smooth)` -- exponentially
  smooths the raw efficiency over the smoothing period; a smooth
  of 1 leaves the raw value untouched.
- `runLinePfe` -- sorts the finite points by x, runs the pipeline,
  and returns per-bar samples classified `up` / `down` / `choppy`
  against the threshold, the zone counts and the final reading.
- `computeLinePfeLayout` -- stacks the price panel above a PFE
  panel on a fixed -100..+100 scale, with the indicator line, one
  marker per defined bar coloured by zone, and dashed upper / zero
  / lower reference lines.
- `describeLinePfeChart` -- an off-screen sentence summary for
  assistive technology.

## Testability

The PFE divides by an irrational sum of segment lengths, so it is
transcendental in general. The tests anchor it three ways:

- A flat price is exact: the straight line equals the path, the
  efficiency is exactly 1, and the polarised reading is `+100` --
  asserted with `toEqual`.
- A constant-slope ramp keeps the irrational `sqrt(slope^2 + 1)`
  factor in both the straight line and the path, where it cancels;
  the efficiency is `1` to floating-point precision -- asserted
  with `toBeCloseTo`.
- A there-and-back move (up then back down to the start) has a
  straight distance of just the horizontal span, giving an exact
  closed form `100 / sqrt(10)` for the period-2 case.

The EMA smoothing is checked against its recurrence, and zone
classification against the threshold.
