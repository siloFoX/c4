## Indicator

Richard Donchian's channel frames the price's recent trading
range. It needs the high and low, so `ChartLineDonchianPoint`
carries `{x, high, low, close}` -- high and low feed the bands,
the close draws the price line.

- `computeLineDonchianUpper(bars, period)` -- the upper band: the
  highest high over each trailing window of `period` bars.
- `computeLineDonchianLower(bars, period)` -- the lower band: the
  lowest low over the same window.
- `computeLineDonchianMiddle(bars, period)` -- the middle band:
  the midpoint of the upper and lower bands.
- `runLineDonchian` -- sorts the finite bars by x, runs the
  pipeline, and returns per-bar samples classified `upper` /
  `lower` / `mid` by the close against the midline, the zone
  counts and the final band readings; `ok` is false for fewer
  than two bars.
- `computeLineDonchianLayout` -- overlays the price line, the
  three band lines and a filled channel area on a single panel,
  with one marker per classified bar.
- `describeLineDonchianChart` -- an off-screen sentence summary
  for assistive technology.

## Testability

The Donchian Channel is a rolling maximum of the high and a
rolling minimum of the low, with the middle band their midpoint
-- all integer max / min / halving operations, no transcendental
and no division beyond a halving. So an integer OHLC fixture
makes the whole pipeline exact, asserted with `toEqual` (an odd
band sum simply lands the midpoint on a `.5`, still an exact
binary fraction). The fixture places the closes above, below and
exactly on the channel midpoint so the upper, lower and mid zones
all appear.
