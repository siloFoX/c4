## Summary

New `ChartLineEmv` UI primitive at
`web/src/components/ui/chart-line-emv.tsx` -- a pure-SVG line chart
with an Ease of Movement panel relating price change to volume. The
top panel renders the midpoint line with a shaded high-low band
behind it; the bottom panel renders the Ease of Movement line on a
symmetric y-axis with a dashed zero centerline and sign-coloured
markers.

This primitive consumes the richest point shape of the chart-line
family -- `{ x, high, low, volume }` -- since the Ease of Movement
combines the bar range, the midpoint move, and volume.

## Indicator math

Richard Arms' Ease of Movement, with an SMA period (default 14) and a
volume divisor (default 1):

```
midpoint[i]     = (high[i] + low[i]) / 2
distanceMoved   = midpoint[i] - midpoint[i-1]
range[i]        = high[i] - low[i]
boxRatio        = (volume[i] / divisor) / range[i]
emv1[i]         = distanceMoved / boxRatio
                = distanceMoved * range * divisor / volume
emv             = SMA(emv1, smaPeriod)
```

`computeLineEmvRaw(highs, lows, volumes, volumeDivisor)` computes the
raw one-period EMV. Index 0 has no prior midpoint so it reads `null`;
a zero volume reads `0` (no division by zero); a negative-zero result
is normalised to positive zero.

`computeLineEmvSma(src, period)` is a simple moving average that
tolerates the leading `null` of the raw series -- each index whose
window of `period` values is fully defined reads their mean.

The Ease of Movement rises when price advances on light volume and
falls when price drops on light volume, swinging around zero.

### Worked fixture

`highs = [12,16,22,20,14]`, `lows = [8,12,18,16,10]`,
`volumes` all `8`, `smaPeriod = 2`, `volumeDivisor = 1`:

```
midpoint = [10, 14, 20, 18, 12]    range = [4, 4, 4, 4, 4]
emv1     = [null, 2, 3, -1, -3]    (distanceMoved * range / volume)
emv      = [null, null, 2.5, 1, -2]   (SMA of emv1, period 2)
```

`emvFinal = -2`, `emvMin = -2`, `emvMax = 2.5`, two readings above and
one below zero.

## API

- `getLineEmvFinitePoints(points)` -- drop points with a non-finite
  `x`, `high`, `low` or `volume`.
- `normalizeLineEmvPeriod(period, fallback)` -- positive integer;
  non-finite / sub-1 falls back, fractional floors.
- `computeLineEmvSma(src, period)` -- the null-tolerant SMA.
- `computeLineEmvRaw(highs, lows, volumes, volumeDivisor)` -- the raw
  one-period EMV series (`(number | null)[]`).
- `computeLineEmv(highs, lows, volumes, smaPeriod, volumeDivisor)` --
  returns `{ midpoint, range, emv1, emv }`.
- `runLineEmv(points, options?)` -- sort finite points by `x`, compute
  the series, return `{ series, smaPeriod, volumeDivisor, midpoint,
  range, emv1, emv, samples, emvFinal, emvMin, emvMax, positiveCount,
  negativeCount, ok }`. Each sample carries a `positive` / `negative`
  / `zero` sign from the smoothed EMV. `ok` is false for fewer than
  two points.
- `computeLineEmvLayout(options)` -- stack a price panel above an
  Ease of Movement panel with a symmetric y-bound. Returns panels,
  ticks, the midpoint path, the high-low band path, the EMV path,
  sign-coloured markers, and the zero centerline `y`.
- `describeLineEmvChart(data, options?)` -- ARIA description.

## Component

`ChartLineEmv` is a `forwardRef` region (`role="region"`) with an
ARIA-linked description, a `role="img"` SVG, panel labels, a config
badge (SMA period), a two-series legend (Midpoint / EMV) with toggle,
a shaded high-low band, the midpoint line, a dashed zero centerline,
the Ease of Movement line, markers coloured by sign, a hover/focus
tooltip exposing the high, low, midpoint, volume, raw EMV, smoothed
EMV, and sign, and a `motion-safe:animate-fade-in` entrance. Every
node carries a `data-section` attribute.

## Distinctness

- **vs chart-line-force-index** -- the Force Index *multiplies* the
  price change by volume; the Ease of Movement *divides* the midpoint
  move by volume (via the box ratio). The Force Index measures the
  power behind a move; the EMV measures how *easily* price moves --
  high readings mean a large move on light volume.
- **vs chart-line-obv** -- On-Balance Volume is a cumulative running
  total of signed volume. The EMV is not cumulative and the volume
  appears in the denominator, not as the accumulated quantity.
- **vs chart-line-mass-index** -- both consume high-low data, but the
  Mass Index never uses volume and sums an EMA ratio; the EMV divides
  the midpoint move by a volume-derived box ratio. The EMV is the
  only chart-line primitive consuming `{ x, high, low, volume }`.
