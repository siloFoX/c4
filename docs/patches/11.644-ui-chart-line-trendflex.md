## Summary

New `ChartLineTrendflex` UI primitive at
`web/src/components/ui/chart-line-trendflex.tsx` -- a pure-SVG
two-panel chart with an Ehlers Trendflex indicator measuring
trend strength from a Super Smoother.

## Indicator math

The Ehlers Trendflex reads trend strength off a Super Smoother
in three stages.

- `computeLineTrendflexSmoother` -- the Ehlers Super Smoother of
  the price, a two-pole low-pass filter
  (`computeLineTrendflexSmootherCoefficients`). The Trendflex
  runs the smoother at half the period; with
  `arg = 1.414*PI / (0.5*period)` the coefficients are
  `a1 = exp(-arg)`, `b1 = 2*a1*cos(arg)`, `c2 = b1`,
  `c3 = -(a1*a1)`, `c1 = 1 - c2 - c3`, and
  `filt[i] = c1*(price[i]+price[i-1])/2 + c2*filt[i-1] +
  c3*filt[i-2]`.

- `computeLineTrendflexSum` -- the trend sum:

```
sum[i] = (1/period) * SUM[count=1..period] (filt[i] - filt[i-count])
```

  Equivalently, the filtered value minus the mean of the previous
  `period` filtered values: positive while the smoother is
  rising, negative while it is falling. Null until the lookback
  window is full.

- `computeLineTrendflex` -- the sum is normalized by the square
  root of an adaptively accumulated mean square:

```
ms[i]        = 0.04 * sum[i]^2 + 0.96 * ms[i-1]
trendflex[i] = ms[i] != 0 ? sum[i] / sqrt(ms[i]) : 0
```

  The result is a zero-centred oscillator: a large positive
  reading marks a strong uptrend, a large negative reading a
  strong downtrend, a reading near zero no trend.

### Worked fixture

The Super Smoother coefficients come from `exp` and `cos` and
are irrational, so the pipeline values are pinned by the
recursions themselves plus exact anchors. The trend sum is
exact-testable on its own: fed the linear ramp
`[0,1,2,3,4,5,6,7,8]` with `period 4`, every filtered value
sits a constant 2.5 above the mean of its four predecessors, so
`computeLineTrendflexSum` returns `[null,null,null,null, 2.5,
2.5, 2.5, 2.5, 2.5]`. An all-zero price series stays at zero
through the whole pipeline.

## API

- `getLineTrendflexFinitePoints(points)` -- drop points with a
  non-finite `x` or `value`.
- `normalizeLineTrendflexPeriod(period, fallback)` -- coerce to
  an integer of at least 2.
- `computeLineTrendflexSmootherCoefficients(period)` -- the
  `{ a1, b1, c1, c2, c3 }` Super Smoother coefficients.
- `computeLineTrendflexSmoother(values, period)` -- the Super
  Smoother of the price.
- `computeLineTrendflexSum(filt, period)` -- the trend sum, null
  through the warm-up.
- `computeLineTrendflex(values, period)` -- the
  `{ filt, sum, ms, trendflex }` series.
- `runLineTrendflex(points, options?)` -- sort finite points by
  `x`, run the pipeline, return `{ series, period, filt, sum, ms,
  trendflex, samples, trendflexFinal, trendflexMin, trendflexMax,
  upCount, downCount, ok }`. Each sample is classified `up` /
  `down` / `flat` by the sign of the Trendflex. `ok` is false for
  fewer than two points.
- `computeLineTrendflexLayout(options)` -- stack the price panel
  above a zero-centred Trendflex panel; project the price and
  Trendflex paths plus markers and the zero line.
- `describeLineTrendflexChart(data, options?)` -- ARIA
  description.

## Component

`ChartLineTrendflex` is a `forwardRef` region (`role="region"`)
with an ARIA-linked description, a `role="img"` SVG, two stacked
panels (price on top, Trendflex below) with their own axis ticks
and grid, panel labels, a config badge showing the period, a
two-series legend (Price / Trendflex) with toggle, a hover/focus
tooltip exposing the value, filter, sum, Trendflex and trend, and
a `motion-safe:animate-fade-in` entrance. It consumes
`{ x, value }` points. The Trendflex panel carries a dashed zero
line and trend-coloured markers (green up, red down, grey flat).
Every node carries a `data-section` attribute.

## Distinctness

- **vs chart-line-roofing** -- the Roofing filter is a band-pass
  in the frequency sense (a high-pass plus a smoother). The
  Trendflex measures trend strength: the average displacement of
  a single Super Smoother, RMS-normalized.
- **vs chart-line-pmo** -- the PMO is a double-smoothed rate of
  change. The Trendflex sums the smoother's displacement
  directly and normalizes by an adaptive mean square.
- **vs the band primitives (stddev / envelope)** -- those draw a
  band around the price. The Trendflex is a zero-centred
  oscillator on its own sub-panel.
